Louisiana Act 19: A New Mandate for Safer Roads & Lower Costs

This interactive guide breaks down Louisiana House Bill 549 (Act 19). The law creates a mandatory insurance discount for commercial vehicles, including municipal fleets, that are equipped with both a dashboard camera and a telematics system. The goal is to enhance public safety, reduce fraud, and lower claim costs for everyone.

The Dual Benefit for Your Municipality

Act 19 presents a clear opportunity to achieve two critical goals: significant financial savings and dramatically improved risk management. This section helps you visualize the potential impact on your budget and operations.

What You'll Need to Comply

To qualify for the mandatory discount, each commercial vehicle must be equipped with *both* of the following systems, meeting specific technical standards.

📹

Dashboard Camera

  • •Dashboard-mounted and forward-facing.
  • •Minimum 1080p resolution.
  • •Continuous loop recording capability.
📡

Telematics System

  • •Collects & transmits real-time driving data.
  • •Tracks speed, braking, and mileage.
  • •Must be compatible with the dash cam for data verification.

Who Does What?

The law establishes clear roles and responsibilities for municipalities, insurers, and the state. Click each tab to understand the specific obligations for each group.

Your Responsibilities as a Policyholder

  • Install and maintain compliant dash cam and telematics systems on all relevant fleet vehicles.
  • Provide proof of installation and operation to your insurer (e.g., vendor certificate).
  • Ensure the systems are in continuous use throughout the policy period.
  • Annually, submit a telematics summary report or a signed affidavit to your insurer to verify ongoing compliance.
  • Failure to maintain the systems will result in losing the discount at the next renewal.

Insurer Responsibilities

  • Provide an actuarially justified liability premium discount for all compliant commercial vehicles.
  • Annually verify that policyholders remain in compliance with the technology requirements.
  • By March 1st each year, report to the Commissioner of Insurance on the number of discounted policies, total savings, and observed changes in claim data.
  • Face penalties for willful noncompliance with the law.

Commissioner of Insurance Responsibilities

  • Establish and publish the minimum technical standards for dash cams and telematics systems.
  • Create rules for verification procedures and potential exemptions.
  • Compile annual reports from all insurers into a summary for the state legislature.
  • The first summary report to the legislature is due by June 1, 2027.

Implementation Timeline & Strategic Action Plan

Act 19 has a defined rollout schedule. Use this timeline to guide your planning and the checklist below to ensure your municipality is prepared.

Now - 2025: Planning & Preparation

This is the window for strategic planning, budgeting, and technology procurement.

January 1, 2026: Act Becomes Effective

The law applies to all commercial motor vehicle insurance policies issued or renewed on or after this date.

March 1, 2027: First Insurer Reports Due

Insurers must submit their first annual report on discount adoption and impact to the Commissioner.

June 1, 2027: First Legislative Report Due

The Commissioner of Insurance submits the first summary report to the House and Senate Insurance Committees.
